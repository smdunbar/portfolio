01. Overview

Welcome to the Redesign project for Allara Health!

Our team collaborated to redesign the dashboard for Allara Health: a virtual care platform specializing in women's hormonal and metabolic health, particularly, conditions like polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)

Our goal was to create an experience that not only informs but empowers users to take control of their healthcare. I approached the project as both a creative storyteller and an user-centered thinker, constantly shifting between empathy and problem-solving.

Understanding Stakeholder Needs

In order to create a design that resonates with users, we first needed to understand their situations, needs, and pain points. Through the project brief, we learned that patients are often overwhelmed by the amount of information presented to them. They are also unsure of how to navigate the platform and where to find important information. Often, patients are left confused and frustrated, leading to a lack of engagement with the platform or discontinuation of care altogether. The goal for Allara is to address these concerns in order to increase patient engagement and retention, by creating a platform that is intuitive, supportive, and empowering.

03. Analyze Existing Dashboard

After blind-ideating, we all took a look at the existing interface and put ourselves in the shoes of an Allara patient to develop a few more pain points.

Existing Functionality

Much of the existing dashboard is concentrated on a single page that contains all the information a patient needs to know. The website includes:

Section Details
Static Care Timeline Informational block for expectation setting (disappears after four appointments, collapsable)
To-Dos Collect information from patients: Share home address, lab preferences, join appointments, download mobile app, review nutrition materials
Appointments Modify appointments, add details to calendar, access provider notes post-appointment
My Health View past appointments, book follow-up visits, see lab orders/results, download PDFs, track progress (e.g., walking 10,000 steps, drinking two liters of water)
Booking Appointments Medical follow-up, nutrition follow-up, confirm state, see availability, book with dietician
Messaging Message concierge team and providers, start new threads, select topics, concierge team routes messages to providers or answers them

The new pain points that we had to account in our redesign were:

  • - Chaotic nature and oversaturation of information on the dashboard
    • - Unclear navigation and lack of clear CTAs (Call to Action)
  • - Users being unsure on where to proceed and how to join appointments

04. Wireframe & Prototyping
Wireframes
The next step in our process was to create wireframes based on the sketches and pain points we had identified. We wanted to create a design that was modular and scannable.

Julia and Bohdan created the main dashboard page, Wes created the To-dos pages, and I created the appointment pages.
 
Changes Justification
Dashboard separated into three main blocks: appointments, to-dos, and lab results. To make the dashboard more scannable and clearly marked.
Timeline moved to the bottom with expandable buttons for new patients. To prioritize CTAs rather than the static timeline and create more interaction and understaning of the patient's current status.
In each appointment you can modify appointments, add details to calendar, access provider notes, and access join button (if upcoming) To make the main appointments more scannable and clear, all specifics details and actions are own their own page for each appointment.
Easy access to different sections. To make the dashboard more interactable and user-friendly.
Navigation toggle between upcoming and past appointments To make it easier for users to find the information they need and navigate to old appointment notes faster.
Task progress bar and reward system To gamify the process and encourage users to complete their tasks.
Stakeholder Feedback
Liv Jenks, the Product Manager from Allara, and Caroline Walters, a Portfolio Operations Manager at GV, commented on our wireframe design and provided essential feedback!

Positive changes

Modular design is great and makes the layout more approachable

Moving the timeline is a good idea, but requires additional work

The addition of progression is amazing

Things to note

Need to have a more forward-looking approach with a dynamic timeline

No appointments are ever in person

Lab results are rather infrequent, so they should not be prioritized on the dashboard

Final Prototype

After receiving a lot of positive feedback as well as pointers to work on, we felt excited to implement our ideas in Figma. Using her feedback, we made the following changes/additions to construct our final prototype.

Changes Justification
Replace lab results with goal tracking Since lab results are infrequent, we decided to replace them with a goal tracking system that allows patients to track their progress and set new goals.
Move to-dos to the top of the page To prioritize the completion of tasks through visual hierarchy.
Added a join option to every appointment To account for the fact that there will never be an in person appointment.
Differentiate detail pop ups for upcoming and past appointments To offer proper information to the user and make it clear what is upcoming and what is past.


Together, we developed an interactive Figma prototype to convey our vision for Allara's patients. We each prototyped the pages we drafted for the wireframe.

05. Conclusion

Approaching the Allara health dashboard redesign iteratively allowed our team to stay closely aligned with user needs while adapting to the complex nuances of women's chronic health management. Through a cycle of research, prototyping, testing, and refinement, we were able to uncover key insights about user behavior, expectations, and pain points—many of which may have been overlooked in a more linear process.

This method fostered meaningful collaboration with Allara product personnel and Google Ventures, resulting in a dashboard experience that feels more intuitive, supportive, and empowering. Ultimately, the iterative approach enabled us to balance usability with empathy, creating a design that truly reflects Allara’s mission of accessible, clear, patient-centered care.

I learned the importance of user-centered design and the value of collaboration in creating a successful product. I also learned how to balance creativity with practicality, and how to communicate effectively with stakeholders.
